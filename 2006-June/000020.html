Hi Maarten and friends,
Now that MRS_2.0 is  nicely indexing embl_release in less than 30 
hours,and while Maarten is finishing up the website part of the soft, it 
is time to start thinking about how to let partners take profit of the 
modularity of MRS. MRS can be configured to build any databank in a 
number of sections (32 at this time for embl, in order not to overload 
the memory). By default, these sections are wiped away after merging on 
the primary building machine. But this can be prevented (just comment 
out the "rm" line in the corresponding script !). Having these sections 
on a peer-to-peer filesystem might be a great exercice in sharing. While 
Uni. Karlruhe is building a _secured_ P2P filesystem, public solutions 
like BitTorrent is a tempting approach suggested by David. What do you 
think, David and George ? And Lubos, are you on the list ?

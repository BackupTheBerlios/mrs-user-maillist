Koen Cuelenaere wrote:
  > prio: 4
>> - linking
>> 	The SRS way of linking results from one databank to records in another.
>>   
> prio: 4
> What I would like to have here, is not (like SRS) have just one way of 
> linking 2 databanks, but several
> ways of linking them... That also means expanding the query language to 
> be able to express what  link route to
> use... Also, one thing that would not be needed from my point of view, 
> is the "shortest route" type of linking,
> where you can link database A to Z while there are no direct links 
> available and the system could get
> their using a shortest route approach. Reason is, often you don't end up 
> with the things you want; so you
> just end up writing out the route in the query language from hop to hop...

I am thinking of something similar for EMBOSS (using MRS as database access 
would make this easy - some others we'd have to hand craft).

Syntax is certainly an issue - we use SRS syntax at present but it can't 
cope with multiple links.

I considered naming the links and including the link name in the syntax. It 
becomes complicated with multiple databases and multiple links so link 
names may need to be reused where there are obvious combinations (embl and 
updates to uniprot and updates for example)

I agree about the shortest route ... from uniprot in 2 hops to another 
database there are often far too many choices and link weighting is a pain.

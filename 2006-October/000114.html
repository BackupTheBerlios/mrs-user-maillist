On Wed, Oct 04, 2006 at 10:08:23AM +0200, Maarten L. Hekkelman wrote:
> Adding a case sensitive index is possible and not that much work.

I think you should put this in your list of things-to-do. Most of the 
time case-insensitive searches are the best, but there are few cases 
where we might need a case-sensitive index for the primary key (ID) 
(like for the PDB sequences) or for some other indexes (I cannot just 
right now find an example). There must then be a way, in the parser 
script or elsewhere, to explicitly state that a certain index must be 
case-sensitive.

> The reason to allow multiple ID's by rewriting the duplicates is that  
> it is very frustrating to have indexing fail for a databank that  
> contains a duplicate ID. Esp. if it is e.g. Genbank and it took you  
> three days to find out there was a duplicate ID. The current solution  
> allows you to at least find the data even though the returned ID's  
> don not make much sense.

Certainly a good idea to avoid MRS crashing on spurious duplicate ID's.

As far as GenBank is concerned : duplicate ID's are a chronic disease, 
they seem not to stick to their own rule of having the ID's unique. A 
solution is to use the accession number as primary key. Is it allowed in 
MRS to call the primary key index by another name than 'id' ? 

> Rewriting different case in ID's to something like LC_id can be done  
> in the parser scripts. No change is needed in MRS itself to allow for  
> this. Though I get the feeling that you trying to find a workaround  
> for a problem that should be solved elsewhere.

I surely agree with that.

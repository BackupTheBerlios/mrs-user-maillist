On Mon, Nov 27, 2006 at 12:01:24PM +0100, mrs-user-request at lists.berlios.de wrote:
>    1. The ID's case (Maarten L. Hekkelman)
> As has been discussed before on this list, mrs is case insensitive  
> for all indices. I believe some of you prefer case sensitive indices,  
> or at least would like to see the ability to add them, right? Is it  
> essential to have this?

We are all used to case-insensitive search engines and most of the time 
case-insensitive searching is the best since we are not sure about the 
correct spelling anyway. There are a few cases where case-sensitivity is 
wished for. I remember the case of the PDB individual sequences where 
****_A and ****_a can be the identifiers of different sequences. The 
best would be that case-insensitivity is the default, but that you can 
request at indexing time a case-sensitive ID or a case-sensitive index 
for a particular field and that at search time you can set case-sensitive 
searching, just as you can set automated wild-card addition.

Did mrs-ws end up being unresponsive? And do you perhaps know which part 
of mrs-ws was using all the memory? If you do a 'ps xgauf' you'll see 
the process hierarchy as well as lots of info on the memory and cpu 
usage of each process. It is often quite easy to find out which forked 
mrs-ws is doing what.

I don't believe we had problems with MRS during courses recently. I 
think we also have more than 20 students per course. If MRS fails, I'm 
the first to know but it's a long time since I had the last emergency 
phone call from a classroom. Now if mrs-ws runs out of memory, it simply 
crashes and is automatically relaunched. That may have happened and 
often no one notices this.

The software that gave the most problems up until now was Tomcat and 
since we replaced it with glassfish I must say things seem to be much 
more stable.

It might be that mrs-ws leaks memory, but I believe what you saw was 
caching of blast results as well as memory mapping of parts of the index 
of mrs files.
